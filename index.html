My biography

Faraaz is a Queensland-trained specialist anaesthetist who places modern, evidence-based practice at the centre of his care. He is committed to providing safe, high-quality anaesthesia and excellent clinical management for every patient.

Faraaz graduated in 2012 from the University of London with two Distinctions, four Merits, and a Prize for Academic Excellence. He initially undertook specialist General Practice training and worked as a GP in both the United Kingdom and the Moreton Bay region, caring for patients in a busy private practice. In addition to his Fellowships, Faraaz holds five postgraduate diplomas, with additional qualifications in older adult medicine, tropical medicine and hygiene, and pre-hospital and retrieval medicine. He is also a Senior Lecturer with the Griffith University School of Medicine.

After choosing to specialise in anaesthesia, Faraaz was accepted into the Queensland Central Rotation for anaesthetic training and worked across major hospitals in Brisbane, the Sunshine Coast, and regional Queensland. He maintains a strong commitment to providing care in regional and remote communities and holds an appointment as an Aeromedical Consultant with the Royal Flying Doctor Service (Queensland).

Faraaz brings a holistic approach to patient care, shaped by his background in general practice and complemented by the rigour of specialist anaesthetic practice. He values clear communication and collaborative care, working closely with patients and surgeons to deliver the highest standards of care.
